## Post 3 — Educational: Turmeric + Blood Thinners

⚠️ **The herb everyone loves... and the drug interaction nobody talks about.**

Turmeric (curcumin) is one of the most popular herbal supplements out there. Anti-inflammatory. Antioxidant. Studied for arthritis, metabolic health, even mood.

But here's the thing: turmeric also has **mild anticoagulant effects**. If you're on warfarin, clopidogrel, or aspirin — this combination can increase your bleeding risk.

This doesn't mean you can't take turmeric. It means you should know the facts before you do.

That's exactly why we built HerbAlly's interaction checker: so you can see, for *your* medications, which herbs have known interactions — and how serious they are.

## Post 4 — Educational: St. John's Wort

🚨 **The one herb that can make your medications stop working.**

St. John's Wort is well-known for mild depression. But many people don't know it's a powerful **CYP3A4 enzyme inducer** — meaning it speeds up how fast your liver breaks down certain drugs.

The result? Your medications become less effective. This affects:

- Birth control pills (unplanned pregnancy risk)
- Antidepressants (SSRIs/MAOIs — serotonin syndrome risk)
- Blood thinners (warfarin)
- HIV medications
- Organ transplant anti-rejection drugs

HerbAlly flags interactions like these with severity ratings — from Mild to Contraindicated — and every claim is sourced from WHO monographs and peer-reviewed research.

## Post 5 — The Dosage Calculator

👶 **"How much of this herb do I give my child?" — A question every parent should ask.**

Adult doses don't scale down to kids. A 6-year-old isn't just a smaller adult — their metabolism, liver enzymes, and body composition are fundamentally different.

HerbAlly's dosage calculator uses four medically recognized formulas:

📐 **Clark's Rule** — weight-based, best for children 2+  
📐 **Young's Rule** — age-based, simple and quick  
📐 **BSA (Mosteller)** — most accurate, uses height + weight  
📐 **Fried's Rule** — specifically for infants under 2

Plug in the adult dose and your child's age/weight. You get a calculated safe dose instantly.

But as always: this is a starting point. Always confirm with your pediatrician.

## Post 8 — The Evidence Grading System

📚 **Not all herbal claims are created equal. Here's how we grade them:**

At HerbAlly, every claim gets an evidence level:

🟢 **Level A — Strong Evidence:** Multiple randomized controlled trials and systematic reviews. (Example: ginger for pregnancy nausea)

🟡 **Level B — Moderate Evidence:** Some clinical studies, but limited by size or quality. (Example: ashwagandha for stress)

🟠 **Level C — Limited Evidence:** Traditional use or preclinical data, minimal human trials.

🔴 **Level D — Anecdotal:** Historical use or theoretical basis only. We flag these clearly.

Herbs are powerful. But not all information about them is equal. We help you tell the difference.

👉 Explore the evidence yourself: [herbally.app/herbs](https://herbally.app/herbs)

## Post 9 — Quick Tip: Ashwagandha

🌙 **Thinking about trying ashwagandha for stress? Here's what you should know:**

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) is an adaptogen with Level B evidence for reducing stress and anxiety. Clinical trials show it can lower cortisol levels by 14–28%.

What to watch for:
- 🕐 It takes 2–4 weeks of consistent use for full effect
- 🩺 Can interact with thyroid medications, sedatives, and immunosuppressants
- 🤰 Not recommended during pregnancy
- 📉 Doses in studies range from 300–600mg of standardized extract (withanolides)

As always: talk to your doctor before starting, especially if you're on meds.
